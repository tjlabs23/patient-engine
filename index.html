Quick word on the terms

Lead — someone who filled out the form or called from the ad.
Cost per lead — what you paid to get one to raise their hand.
Booking rate — of the leads, how many booked an appointment.
Show rate — of the booked, how many actually showed up.
New-patient value — first visit + care plan, averaged (~$1,300 here).
Return — the headline. Patient revenue ÷ ad spend. "$1 in → $X back."

Watch the gap between cheap leads and low show-rate: it means people are interested but not making it in the door. That's a follow-up problem, not an ad problem.

6

Creative report card — and what to make next

The single biggest driver of cheaper patients isn't the audience — it's the creative. Here's what your ads are telling us, and the exact new pieces to shoot.

✓ What's winning
  • Real patient stories (UGC video). A person in your chair beats a polished clinic ad every time — it's trust, not production.
  • Hyper-specific pain hooks. "That sharp pain down your leg" outperforms "back & neck care" — name the exact symptom and the right person self-selects.
  • Pattern-interrupt openers. "Were you in a car accident in the last 30 days?" stops the scroll because it's a yes/no question aimed at one person.
✕ What's flopping
  • Generic clinic graphics. Logo + stock spine + "Sciatica Treatment Available" reads like an ad, so people scroll past it.
  • Vague CTAs. "Book today," "Your health matters," "Now accepting patients" — no symptom, no reason, no urgency.
  • Insurance-led hooks. "We take most insurance" attracts price-shoppers who don't show — the worst-quality lead on the account.
Make these next — 5 concepts, prioritized
UGC Video

"I canceled my back surgery after 3 visits."

Real patient, phone-shot. Opens on the sentence, then the story. Target: chronic back pain & disc. Why: story + specific outcome = the highest-trust format you have.

Reel

"The 20-second test to check for a pinched nerve."

Doctor demos a quick self-test. Educational, save-worthy, ends on "if that hurt, come see us." Why: teaches first, sells second — earns the click and the save.

UGC Video

"Rear-ended last week? Do this before you feel fine."

Urgency + education for auto-injury (your 21x campaign). Why: PI is your highest-value patient — feed the winner with more of what works.

Reel

"3 sleep positions making your neck pain worse."

Fast, visual, list-style. Broad top-of-funnel reach that still qualifies. Why: problem-aware hook pulls people who don't know they need you yet.

Static / Offer

"$29 — Exam, X-rays & your first adjustment."

Clean offer card, no clutter, one clear button. Why: your proven offer works — it just needs a crisp, specific execution, not a generic graphic.
